About The Smoky Mountains

The Great Smoky Mountains, a subrange of the Appalachian Mountains, are an emblem of natural beauty and rich history, straddling the border between North Carolina and Tennessee. Known for their mist-covered peaks, which give the mountains their name, they are home to a diverse array of flora and fauna, some of which are found nowhere else on Earth. The ancient mountains boast a wondrous diversity of life, with over 19,000 documented species. The park preserves the heritage of Southern Appalachian mountain culture and is the most visited national park in the United States. This is a testament to its breathtaking landscapes, from cascading waterfalls to expansive forests and an array of historic buildings. Visitors to the Great Smoky Mountains can explore a multitude of trails, including a portion of the famed Appalachian Trail, which traverses the park from Maine to Georgia. The park offers a variety of experiences, from auto touring along the scenic Newfound Gap Road to wildlife viewing, particularly the park's population of black bears. The park's commitment to preservation is evident in its designation as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an International Biosphere Reserve. Whether it's the serene beauty of Cades Cove or the panoramic views from Clingmans Dome, the highest point in Tennessee, the Great Smoky Mountains National Park is a place where nature's grandeur is on full display, inviting adventurers and tranquility seekers alike to bask in its majestic beauty.

Food

No campout or RV trip is complete without the delight of good food! Imagine sitting by a crackling fire under the open sky, the aroma of freshly grilled vegetables and sizzling steaks wafting through the air. It's not just about sustenance; it's about the experience. Cooking outdoors turns a meal into an event, bringing together friends and family for a feast that's as much about the laughter and stories shared around the fire as it is about the food on the plate. Whether it's the simplicity of hot dogs and marshmallows or the sophistication of a gourmet spread, the right food can turn any outdoor excursion into a memorable adventure.

Camp Recipes

Cooking in the Smokies can be a delightful experience, allowing you to enjoy the great outdoors while preparing delicious meals. One could consider simple yet hearty recipes like foil-wrapped veggies and meats that can be cooked directly over a campfire. Another idea is to prepare skewers with a variety of meats, vegetables, and even fruits for a quick and customizable meal option. For breakfast, campfire pancakes or scrambled eggs can start the day off right.

Local Option

While there may not be many resturants tucked in the Smoky Mountains, Gatlinburg sits conviniently right outside of the park. It offers a diverse culinary scene that caters to all tastes and preferences. From the cozy cabin setting of Delauder's BBQ, known for its divine smoked meats, to the historic ambiance of The Peddler Steakhouse, visitors can indulge in a range of flavors. Pancake Pantry, a local favorite, serves up what many consider the best pancakes around, while those looking for a high-end dining experience might prefer the exclusive offerings of The Greenbrier. Each restaurant provides not just a meal, but an experience that complements the natural beauty and adventure found in the Smokies. while it may seem odd to drive to Gatlinburg during your outdoor retreat, you will quickly find you were missing out before!

Hiking

The Great Smoky Mountains National Park offers a diverse array of hiking trails that cater to all levels of hikers, each promising a unique and enriching experience amidst nature's splendor. For those seeking breathtaking views, Clingmans Dome is a must-visit, being the highest point in the park and accessible via a short hike. The Alum Cave Trail provides a moderately challenging hike with rewarding vistas and geological features, while the Andrews Bald trail offers an easier hike from Clingmans Dome to a scenic grassy bald. For waterfall enthusiasts, the Abrams Falls Trail and Ramsey Cascades offer stunning waterfall views, with the latter being the tallest in the park. History buffs may enjoy the Cades Cove Loop, which is rich in historical sites and wildlife. Those looking for a more strenuous adventure might tackle the 8-mile round-trip hike on the Appalachian Trail from Newfound Gap to Charlies Bunion, which features some of the most striking panoramic views in the park. Each trail in the Smokies provides a unique opportunity to connect with nature, witness the park's biodiversity, and enjoy the serene beauty of the Appalachian wilderness.

Views

The Great Smoky Mountains, a subrange of the Appalachian Mountains, offer some of the most breathtaking views in the United States. The park is renowned for its diverse wildlife, ancient mountains, and remnants of Southern Appalachian mountain culture. One of the best ways to experience the grandeur of the Smokies is by visiting some of its famous viewpoints. Clingmans Dome, the highest point in Tennessee and the Smokies, provides a panoramic view that spans over 100 miles on clear days. The Newfound Gap, with its lower elevation, offers a different but equally stunning perspective, where visitors can stand on the state line between North Carolina and Tennessee. For those seeking a serene and less crowded experience, the Look Rock Tower offers a 360-degree view of the mountainous landscape. Each viewpoint presents a unique vista that encapsulates the natural beauty of the Smoky Mountains, making it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts and photographers alike. Whether you're driving through the scenic routes or hiking up the trails, the Smokies provide an array of picturesque landscapes that are sure to leave a lasting impression.
